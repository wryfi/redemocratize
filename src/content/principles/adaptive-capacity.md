A constitution's first obligation is ensuring its own revisability and self-correction.

## Revisability

Any fundamental law that cannot be amended by the living political community within a generation is democratically illegitimate. Rigidity is not stability — it is deferred crisis.

## Self-Correction

A well-designed democratic system maintains its own integrity the way a healthy organism maintains homeostasis — through structural resilience that automatically restores function when any component is compromised. This means automatic triggers rather than discretionary ones, distributed enforcement rather than centralized, structural deference decay for compromised institutions, mandatory transparency, and recall and rotation as defaults.

The system's immune response must not depend on the political will of actors who may themselves be compromised.

## Anti-Entrenchment

No government, party, or officeholder may use the powers of office to entrench itself or make itself harder to remove — including gerrymandering, voter suppression, packing courts, changing electoral rules, or weaponizing investigation of opponents.

## Emergency Powers

Emergency declarations automatically sunset on a short timeline, cannot be renewed by the authority that invoked them, and must be ratified by a deliberative body to continue.
