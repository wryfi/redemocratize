---
title: Democratic Sovereignty over Institutions
number: 10
tier: substantive
summary: "No institutional form confers immunity from democratic accountability. Jurisdiction follows impact."
---

No institutional form — corporate, religious, nonprofit, governmental — confers immunity from democratic accountability.

Corporations are legal instruments chartered by the polity for specific purposes, revocable, and bearing no inherent rights against the communities that create them. Jurisdiction follows impact, not incorporation. Accountability scales with an institution's impact on the lives of people subject to it.

This principle dissolves both corporate personhood and religious exemptionalism without targeting either specifically.
